Phase 1: Building Your Foundation

This first phase is about establishing your entry into healthcare. It involves completing the required certifications, understanding the costs and timeline for the CNA program at Edmonds College, and getting ready for the hands-on experience that is foundational to a competitive BSN application.

Mandatory Certifications

These are non-negotiable and must be completed at the right time. They are the first step in your professional journey.

1. Basic Life Support (BLS)

You need the **American Heart Association (AHA) BLS for Healthcare Providers** course. Complete this *before* starting the CNA program. Find local classes at CPR Northwest Washington or Newcastle Training.

2. HIPAA & Bloodborne Pathogens

This training on patient privacy and safety is required for your clinical externship. Edmonds will direct you to their required online provider *after* you enroll.

Phase 3: The UW BSN Application

Gaining admission to UW's BSN program is extremely competitive. The admissions committee uses a holistic review, looking beyond grades to your experience and character. This chart illustrates the significant gap between the minimum requirements and the profile of a competitive, successful applicant.

Minimums vs. A Competitive Profile

While meeting the minimums gets your application reviewed, aiming for the competitive profile is what leads to admission. Hover for details.

Key Application Components

Each piece of your application must tell a cohesive story of your readiness for nursing.

  • ✓
    Strong Science GPA: Aim well above the 3.0 minimum in science prerequisites. This is a key academic filter.
  • ✓
    Extensive Healthcare Experience: CNA work is ideal. Aim for 500-1000+ hours to be truly competitive.
  • ✓
    Compelling Essays: Use a journal from your CNA work to provide specific, reflective stories that answer "Why nursing?".
  • ✓
    Powerful Recommendation: A letter from a nurse supervisor who has seen your work ethic is a "huge asset".
  • ✓
    Leadership & Service: Shows you are a well-rounded individual committed to your community.
  • ✓
    Proctored Essay: Top applicants are invited to this timed essay, testing critical thinking under pressure.

Phase 4: Your Strategic Roadmap & Contingencies

Treating this journey like a multi-year project is the key to success. Use this interactive timeline to visualize your path and review the crucial backup plans needed for this highly competitive field.

A Sample 2-Year Strategic Timeline

Year 1: Foundation & Experience

Fall/Winter/Spring: Start Pre-Nursing DTA/MRP. Meet with an advisor immediately. Focus on core bio/chem courses and maintaining a high GPA.

Summer: Complete the intensive Edmonds CNA program. Pass the state exam and get your NAC license.

Upon Certification: Secure a part-time CNA job to start accumulating high-quality patient care hours immediately.

Year 2: Execution & Application

Click to expand details

Fall/Winter: Finish all remaining prerequisites, especially advanced sciences and the UW-specific logic course. Continue CNA work, aiming for 500+ hours.

Late Fall: Begin drafting your UW BSN application essays. Use a journal from your CNA work for powerful, specific examples.

Deadline (Jan 15): Submit your polished and complete application well before the firm deadline.

Risk Mitigation: Always Have a Backup Plan

Given the low acceptance rate at UW Seattle, a smart strategy involves applying to multiple programs to increase your chances of becoming an RN.

Apply Broadly

The Pre-Nursing DTA/MRP prepares you for many BSN programs. Apply simultaneously to WSU, Seattle U, PLU, and others.

Research Requirements

Double-check prerequisites for your backup schools. Use the UW Equivalency Guide and similar tools for other universities.

Stay Flexible

Be prepared to take an extra course or two if needed to qualify for a wider range of excellent nursing programs.
